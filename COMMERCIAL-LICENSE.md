# Commercial Licensing

The HipAAsynth engine is licensed under the **GNU Affero General Public License,
version 3 (AGPL v3)**. This page explains when a commercial license may be the
right choice. It is an offer, not an obligation — the AGPL v3 grant is, and will
remain, available to everyone at no cost.

## What AGPL v3 requires

Under AGPL v3, any organization that embeds the HipAAsynth engine in a
proprietary or closed-source product — including software offered to users over
a network — must release the complete corresponding source code of their entire
stack under AGPL v3. This is the standard copyleft obligation of the license, and
for many open-source projects and research efforts it is exactly the right fit.

## When a commercial license helps

Some organizations cannot or prefer not to meet the AGPL v3 source-disclosure
obligations — for example, when integrating HipAAsynth into a proprietary
clinical product, a closed SaaS offering, or an internal platform that cannot be
open-sourced.

For those organizations, a **commercial license is available**. It permits
proprietary use of the HipAAsynth engine without the AGPL v3 open-source
requirements, on mutually agreed terms.

A commercial license does not change the open-source project: the AGPL v3 edition
remains fully available. It simply provides an alternative set of terms for
organizations whose use does not fit the AGPL v3 model.

## Certified evaluation packages

Beyond the engine itself, HipAAsynth offers **certified evaluation packages as a
service**, including the proprietary **CAP (cryptographic certification)
pipeline** — a hash-chained, independently verifiable FairnessPassport suitable
for regulatory submissions and hospital procurement. These certification
services are offered separately from, and in addition to, the open-source engine.
