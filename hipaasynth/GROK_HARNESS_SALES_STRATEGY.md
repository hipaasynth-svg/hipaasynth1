## Pricing Strategy (Rationale)

### Why $3,500–$5,500 for Pilot?

1. **Labor cost:** 2–4 hours harness runtime + 2 hours consulting + 2 hours reporting
2. **IP value:** You're giving them proof worth 10x the cost (enables their enterprise deal)
3. **Market anchoring:** Consultants charge $150–$300/hr; you're at ~$400/hr (premium, defensible)
4. **Entry-level:** Low enough to be approved without procurement machinery; high enough to filter tire-kickers

### Why $25k for RIV-Cert?

1. **SaaS mentality:** Quarterly re-runs + custom profiles justify recurring fee
2. **Certification value:** "RIV-Certified" vendor badge = marketing value for you AND them
3. **Enterprise pricing:** Premium segment can pay; watch for expansion revenue

## Objection Handling

### "We already have internal validation."
> "Great. Question: is it auditable by regulators? Is it reproducible on another machine? Does it test across population variation? Most internal validation isn't designed for that. We specialize in *regulatory-ready* proof."

### "Grok seems good enough."
> "Grok is powerful — but 'good enough' for which population? Your urban patients? Your rural patients? Patients with comorbidities? Validation proves that. No surprises at scale."

### "We'll validate it in production."
> "That's expensive and risky. We validate in 2 weeks for $3.5k. Saves you from deploying to patients and discovering failures downstream."

### "This sounds like... synthetic data? We thought you don't do that."
> "Exactly — we don't sell data. We sell validation *services*. We generate synthetic cohorts, you test your model against them, we give you a report. The synthetic data stays local; nothing leaves your environment. Zero PHI exposure."
